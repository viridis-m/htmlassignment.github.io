SUNGEI BULOH WETLAND RESERVE

Mangrove In The City

Located in the Northwest of Singapore, Sungei Buloh was first opened as a Nature Park in 1993. In 2002, the 130 hectares nature reserve was renamed Sungei Buloh Wetland Reserve. In the same year, it was recognised as a site of international importance for migratory birds and awarded a certificate by Wetlands International, marking the reserve's formal entry into the East Asian Australasian Shorebird Site Network. In 2003, Sungei Buloh Wetland Reserve became Singapore's first ASEAN Heritage Park. Since then, it expanded to 202 hectares consisting of mangroves, mudflats, forests and ponds, a home to diverse wildlife and birds.

Step Into The Unknown

BIRD WATCHING

From September to March every year, thousands of migratory birds from the Northern hemisphere fly in to our very own mudflats to tide over the cold winter months. Visiting during the non-migratory period instead? No worries! You will still be warmly welcomed by at least 4 species of Sungei Buloh's residential Kingfishers.

GOING ON A HIKE

There's no other way to discover the beauty and wonders of nature than to wander through the reserve itself. Get lost in any of the 4 magical trails available: Coastal Trail (1300m), Forest Trail (300m), Mid-canopy Walk (150m), Migratory Bird Trail (1950m) and catch a glimpse of its native inhabitants such as mudskippers, crabs, shellfish, water snakes, birds, spiders, monitor lizards and otters.

Essential Tips

  • Sungei Buloh is best enjoyed during sunrise, before the afternoon sun hits (7am - 11.30am)
  • Have an umbrella or poncho on hand
  • Bring along sunblock, insect repellent and plenty of water
  • Dress yourself in comfortable light coloured clothes (preferably long-sleeved shirt and long pants to prevent mosquito bites)
  • Put on comfortable walking shoes with socks
  • Bring a good pair of binoculars and foldable chair for comfortable viewing
  • Should you encounter a saltwater crocodile, back away calmly and call +65 6794 1401 (8.30am - 5.30pm) or 1800-4717-300 (after hours)
